CARBON ACCOUNTING FOR TRANSPORT OPERATIONS
Document ID: KB-CAT-008
Effective Date: 2025-09-01
Regulatory Basis: GHG Protocol Corporate Standard; EPA Emission Factors for GHG Inventories

1. BASE EMISSION FACTORS

The standard emission factor for refrigerated truck transport is 0.12 kg CO2-equivalent
per vehicle-kilometer (vkm). This factor covers Scope 1 direct emissions from diesel
combustion and Scope 2 indirect emissions from auxiliary refrigeration unit power draw.
The factor is derived from EPA emission factors for heavy-duty vehicles and adjusted for
the additional fuel consumption of transport refrigeration units operating at a setpoint
of 4 degrees Celsius.

2. AMBIENT TEMPERATURE DEGRADATION

When ambient temperature exceeds 35 degrees Celsius, refrigeration unit coefficient of
performance (COP) degrades significantly. Under these conditions, the effective emission
factor increases by 40 percent to approximately 0.168 kg CO2-eq per vkm. This degradation
must be applied automatically by the carbon accounting module when ambient temperature
sensor data or regional weather feeds indicate sustained high-temperature conditions.

3. SCOPE CLASSIFICATION

Fuel combustion by owned or leased vehicles falls under Scope 1. Purchased electricity
for electric refrigeration units and depot charging falls under Scope 2. Outsourced
carrier emissions are Scope 3 Category 4 (upstream transportation) per the GHG Protocol
Corporate Value Chain Standard.

4. REQUIRED ACTIONS

Action 1: Calculate per-shipment carbon intensity by multiplying route distance by the
applicable emission factor (base or heat-adjusted). Record the result in the shipment
carbon ledger and attach it to the corresponding blockchain audit record.

Action 2: Aggregate monthly emissions by scope category and submit to the cooperative
sustainability office for inclusion in the annual GHG inventory report. Any month where
total transport emissions exceed the quarterly budget by more than 10 percent must
trigger a corrective action review within 14 days.
