TEMPERATURE EXCURSION RESPONSE PROTOCOL
Document ID: KB-TEX-001
Effective Date: 2025-09-01
Regulatory Basis: FSMA Preventive Controls Rule (21 CFR Part 117)

1. EXCURSION CLASSIFICATION

When cold chain temperature exceeds the 8 degrees Celsius threshold, operators must
classify the excursion severity within 15 minutes of detection:

  - Minor: Temperature between 8-10 degrees Celsius sustained for less than 30 minutes.
    Product may continue in cold chain after verification and documentation.
  - Moderate: Temperature between 10-15 degrees Celsius, OR any excursion exceeding
    30 minutes regardless of peak temperature. Product requires quality hold and
    time-temperature integration analysis before release.
  - Critical: Temperature exceeding 15 degrees Celsius, OR any excursion lasting more
    than 2 hours. Product must be diverted from human consumption pathways immediately.

2. REQUIRED ACTIONS

Action 1: Initiate time-temperature integration (TTI) calculation using the cumulative
thermal exposure model. Record sensor readings at 60-second intervals throughout the
excursion window. Calculate degree-hours above baseline (4 degrees Celsius).

Action 2: Notify the designated food safety officer and log the excursion in the HACCP
deviation record within 30 minutes. For moderate and critical events, quarantine affected
lots and suspend downstream distribution until corrective action review is complete.

3. TTI DECISION THRESHOLDS

If cumulative TTI exceeds 20 degree-hours, reclassify as critical regardless of peak
temperature. Products with TTI below 10 degree-hours and peak below 10 degrees Celsius
may be released with documentation per FSMA Section 117.150 corrective action requirements.
All excursion records must be retained for a minimum of 2 years.
