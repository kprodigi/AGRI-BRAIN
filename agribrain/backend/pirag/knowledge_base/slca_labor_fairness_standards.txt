SOCIAL LIFE CYCLE ASSESSMENT: LABOR FAIRNESS STANDARDS
Document ID: KB-SLF-005
Effective Date: 2025-09-01
Regulatory Basis: ILO Conventions; UNEP-SETAC Guidelines for Social LCA of Products

1. SHIFT DURATION LIMITS BY ROUTING PATHWAY

Cold chain logistics operators are subject to maximum shift durations of 10 to 12 hours,
consistent with the U.S. Department of Transportation Hours of Service regulations
(49 CFR Part 395). Drivers must take a mandatory 30-minute rest break after 8 consecutive
hours of driving. Exceeding the 12-hour ceiling triggers an automatic routing penalty
in the SLCA labor fairness score.

Local redistribution routes, including food bank deliveries and community distribution
center transfers, should be planned within 4 to 6 hour shift windows. This range
balances operational efficiency with reduced worker fatigue and supports equitable
labor conditions as outlined in the ILO Decent Work framework.

2. LABOR FAIRNESS SCORING

The SLCA labor fairness subscore is calculated on a 0 to 1 scale using the following
criteria:

  - Shift length at or below 8 hours: score 1.00
  - Shift length between 8 and 10 hours: score 0.80
  - Shift length between 10 and 12 hours: score 0.60
  - Shift length exceeding 12 hours: score 0.30

3. REQUIRED ACTIONS

Action 1: Record actual shift start and end times for all routing personnel in the
Labor Compliance Log (LCL-500). Cross-reference with GPS telemetry data to verify
reported hours. Discrepancies exceeding 30 minutes must be flagged for supervisory
review within 48 hours.

Action 2: Conduct quarterly labor fairness audits per UNEP-SETAC methodology, including
worker interviews, payroll verification, and rest period compliance checks. Submit
audit summaries to the cooperative governance board for review.
