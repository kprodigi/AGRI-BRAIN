ANIMAL FEED DIVERSION STANDARDS
Document ID: KB-AFD-003
Effective Date: 2025-09-01
Regulatory Basis: AAFCO Model Regulations; FDA 21 CFR Part 589

1. DIVERSION ELIGIBILITY

Product is eligible for diversion to animal feed when the spoilage index rho falls
within the range of 0.30 to 0.60. Product below rho 0.30 should be prioritized for
human consumption or food bank redistribution. Product above rho 0.60 must be routed
to composting, anaerobic digestion, or landfill disposal channels.

2. MICROBIAL TESTING REQUIREMENTS

When rho exceeds 0.45, a microbial screening panel is required before release to any
animal feed processor. The panel must include total aerobic plate count, Salmonella spp.,
and E. coli O157:H7. Testing must be performed by an ISO 17025-accredited laboratory.
Product may not ship until results confirm compliance with AAFCO microbial limits for
the target animal species.

3. PROHIBITED CONDITIONS

The following conditions disqualify product from animal feed use regardless of rho value:
presence of non-food-grade packaging contaminants, chemical adulterants above FDA action
levels, or visible fungal colonization exceeding 15 percent of surface area.

4. REQUIRED ACTIONS

Action 1: Complete the Animal Feed Diversion Certificate (AFDC-300) documenting commodity
type, rho at diversion decision, microbial test status (if applicable), and destination
feed mill or processor. Retain copies for 2 years per AAFCO traceability requirements.

Action 2: Calculate the economic recovery value using the feed value factor of 15 to
25 percent of retail price, adjusted by commodity type and current feed ingredient market
rates. Log the recovery value in the financial reconciliation system for SLCA reporting
and cooperative audit purposes.
