SOCIAL LIFE CYCLE ASSESSMENT: PRICE TRANSPARENCY FRAMEWORK
Document ID: KB-SPT-007
Effective Date: 2025-09-01
Regulatory Basis: Fair Trade USA Standards; UNEP-SETAC Social LCA Guidelines

1. TRANSPARENCY SCORES BY PATHWAY

Each routing pathway is assigned a price transparency score reflecting the degree to
which pricing information is accessible and verifiable by cooperative members and
external auditors:

  - Cold chain (conventional retail): transparency score 0.45. Pricing is partially
    opaque due to multi-tier wholesale intermediaries and confidential retailer margins.
  - Redistribution (food bank/community): transparency score 0.85. Pricing is highly
    visible because transactions occur at or near cost with documented subsidies and
    donation valuations.
  - Recovery (animal feed/composting): transparency score 0.70. Commodity-based pricing
    with publicly available feed ingredient indices, though processing fees introduce
    moderate variability.

2. COOPERATIVE AUDIT RIGHTS

All cooperative member organizations retain the right to request a full price
transparency audit for any routing decision made within the preceding 30 days.
Audit requests must be acknowledged within 5 business days and completed within
20 business days of acknowledgment. This provision aligns with Fair Trade criteria
for producer pricing access and ensures that no member is disadvantaged by opaque
margin structures.

3. REQUIRED ACTIONS

Action 1: Publish a quarterly Price Transparency Report detailing the average
transparency score per pathway, the number of transactions per pathway, total revenue
and cost breakdowns, and any audit findings. Distribute to all cooperative members
within 30 days of quarter close.

Action 2: Implement automated price transparency score calculation within the routing
decision engine. The engine must attach the applicable transparency score to each
routing recommendation and flag any transaction where the effective margin deviates
more than 15 percent from the pathway benchmark for manual review.
