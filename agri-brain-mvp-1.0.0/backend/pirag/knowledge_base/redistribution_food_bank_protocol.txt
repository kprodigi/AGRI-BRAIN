FOOD BANK REDISTRIBUTION PROTOCOL
Document ID: KB-RFB-002
Effective Date: 2025-09-01
Regulatory Basis: FDA FSMA (21 CFR Parts 112, 117); Feeding America Food Safety Guidelines

1. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

Product is eligible for food bank redistribution when the spoilage index rho is below
0.30. This threshold represents product that remains safe and nutritious for human
consumption. Products with rho at or above 0.30 must be diverted to alternative recovery
channels such as animal feed or composting.

Temperature at the point of transfer to the food bank receiving dock must be at or below
7 degrees Celsius for all refrigerated commodities. Any product arriving above this
threshold must be rejected and logged as a temperature non-conformance.

2. TRANSPORT CONSTRAINTS

For leafy greens and cut produce, transport from the originating facility to the food
bank must be completed within 4 hours of initial loading. Refrigerated vehicles must
maintain continuous temperature logging with readings recorded at minimum 5-minute
intervals throughout transit.

3. REQUIRED ACTIONS

Action 1: Complete the Redistribution Transfer Form (RTF-200) including lot number,
commodity description, rho value at time of decision, temperature at loading, estimated
transit duration, and receiving facility identifier. Both the shipping and receiving
parties must sign.

Action 2: Conduct a visual and organoleptic inspection at the receiving dock. Record
results in the Food Bank Intake Log. Reject any units showing visible mold, off-odors,
or packaging compromise. Notify the originating facility of rejections within 24 hours.

Feeding America member organizations must additionally verify compliance with their
Product Sourcing and Safety Standards, including traceability documentation linking
each lot to its origin farm or packing house.
