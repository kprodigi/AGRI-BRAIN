CYBER AND COMMUNICATIONS OUTAGE CONTINGENCY
Document ID: KB-COC-010
Effective Date: 2025-09-01
Regulatory Basis: NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) v2.0; FSMA Preventive Controls Rule

1. SCOPE AND ACTIVATION

This protocol applies when the central decision engine, sensor telemetry network, or
blockchain ledger becomes unavailable due to communications failure, cyberattack, or
infrastructure outage. Activation occurs automatically when the system health monitor
detects no successful heartbeat response for 3 consecutive polling cycles (default cycle
interval: 60 seconds, so activation at approximately 3 minutes of sustained failure).

2. FALLBACK OPERATING PROCEDURES

During a communications outage, all field agents and routing operators must fall back
to cached routing policies. The local policy cache stores the most recent 48 hours of
validated routing decisions and SLCA parameters. Operators must record all product
movements, temperature readings, and routing decisions using manual timestamps on
paper-based Outage Operations Logs (OOL-600).

Within 2 hours of communications restoration, all manual records must be digitized and
synchronized with the central decision engine. The sync process must reconcile cached
decisions with any conflicting updates issued during the outage window.

3. BLOCKCHAIN TRANSACTION HANDLING

Blockchain audit transactions that cannot be submitted during the outage must be queued
locally and batch-submitted upon restoration. Each batched transaction must include the
original decision timestamp, the outage flag indicator, and a hash linking it to the
corresponding manual log entry.

4. REQUIRED ACTIONS

Action 1: Conduct a post-outage incident review within 24 hours of restoration per NIST
CSF Respond (RS) function requirements. Document outage duration, root cause, data gaps,
and any decisions made under degraded conditions.

Action 2: Verify integrity of all batch-submitted blockchain transactions by comparing
on-chain records against manual Outage Operations Logs. Flag and investigate any
discrepancies within 48 hours. Report findings to the cooperative governance board.
