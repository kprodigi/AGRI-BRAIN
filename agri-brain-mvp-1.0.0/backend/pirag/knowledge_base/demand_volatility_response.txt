DEMAND VOLATILITY RESPONSE PROTOCOL
Document ID: KB-DVR-011
Effective Date: 2025-09-01
Regulatory Basis: ISO 31000 Risk Management Framework; FSMA Preventive Controls Rule

1. DETECTION METHODOLOGY

Demand volatility is monitored using a rolling 30-day mean with Bollinger Band analysis.
The volatility threshold is set at k=2.0 standard deviations from the rolling mean. When
observed demand exceeds the upper band (surge condition) or falls below the lower band
(trough condition), the system enters volatility response mode automatically.

The detection algorithm calculates the rolling mean and standard deviation using
exponentially weighted moving averages with a half-life of 7 days to ensure
responsiveness to recent demand shifts while dampening short-term noise.

2. SURGE RESPONSE

When demand exceeds 2 standard deviations above the rolling mean, the routing engine
shifts to conservative mode with the following adjustments: prioritize cold chain
pathways for highest-quality product (rho below 0.15), reduce maximum route length
by 25 percent to minimize transit risk, and increase safety stock allocation at primary
distribution hubs by 20 percent.

3. TROUGH RESPONSE

When demand falls below 2 standard deviations under the rolling mean, surplus product
must be redirected to redistribution channels. Activate food bank redistribution for
all product with rho below 0.30. Accelerate animal feed diversion decisions for product
approaching the 0.30 threshold to prevent spoilage escalation during the low-demand
period.

4. REQUIRED ACTIONS

Action 1: Generate a Demand Volatility Alert Report within 1 hour of threshold breach.
The report must include current demand level, rolling mean, standard deviation, breach
magnitude, and recommended routing adjustments. Distribute to all routing operators and
the cooperative governance board.

Action 2: Conduct a post-event demand variance analysis within 7 days of return to
normal operating range. Document the root cause of volatility, effectiveness of response
actions, product loss or diversion volumes, and recommended parameter adjustments for
future events per ISO 31000 continuous improvement requirements.
