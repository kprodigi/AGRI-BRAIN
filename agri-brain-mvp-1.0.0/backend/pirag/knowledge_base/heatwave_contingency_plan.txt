HEATWAVE CONTINGENCY PLAN
Document ID: KB-HCP-009
Effective Date: 2025-09-01
Regulatory Basis: FEMA Heat Emergency Guidelines; OSHA Heat Illness Prevention Standards

1. ACTIVATION CRITERIA

This contingency plan activates when ambient temperature exceeds 35 degrees Celsius
for 6 or more consecutive hours, as reported by the regional weather monitoring feed
or on-site environmental sensors. Activation is automatic upon threshold detection and
remains in effect until ambient temperature drops below 32 degrees Celsius for at least
2 consecutive hours.

2. OPERATIONAL ADJUSTMENTS

Upon activation, the following modifications to standard routing parameters take effect
immediately:

  - Maximum cold chain transport distance is reduced from the standard 120 kilometers
    to 60 kilometers. This reduction limits cumulative thermal exposure and preserves
    cold chain integrity under degraded refrigeration performance.
  - The secondary food bank redistribution network is activated, routing eligible product
    (rho below 0.30) to the nearest qualified food bank within the reduced distance
    radius rather than holding for long-haul cold chain transport.
  - Inspection frequency at receiving docks increases from the standard interval of
    every 4 hours to every 1 hour. Temperature verification, visual quality checks,
    and packaging integrity assessments must all be performed at the accelerated cadence.

3. REQUIRED ACTIONS

Action 1: Notify all active drivers, warehouse supervisors, and food bank coordinators
of heatwave contingency activation within 30 minutes of trigger detection. Use the
automated alert distribution system with confirmation receipt tracking.

Action 2: Increase cold chain monitoring sensor polling frequency from 5-minute intervals
to 1-minute intervals for the duration of the contingency. Log all readings to the
real-time telemetry dashboard and flag any excursion above 6 degrees Celsius for
immediate investigation per the Temperature Excursion Response Protocol (KB-TEX-001).
