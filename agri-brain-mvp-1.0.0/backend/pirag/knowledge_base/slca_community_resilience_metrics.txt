SOCIAL LIFE CYCLE ASSESSMENT: COMMUNITY RESILIENCE METRICS
Document ID: KB-SCR-006
Effective Date: 2025-09-01
Regulatory Basis: USDA Food Access Research Atlas; UNEP-SETAC Social LCA Guidelines

1. FOOD ACCESS SCORING

Community resilience is assessed using the USDA Food Access Research Atlas to identify
low-access census tracts. Routing decisions that deliver product to areas classified as
low-income and low-access (more than 1 mile from a supermarket in urban areas, or more
than 10 miles in rural areas) receive a food access bonus in the SLCA community
resilience subscore.

Redistribution to underserved communities scores 0.88 on the normalized community
benefit scale. Direct cold chain delivery to conventional retail outlets in well-served
areas scores 0.35. These differential scores ensure the optimization engine prioritizes
equitable food distribution when product quality and logistics constraints permit.

2. ECONOMIC MULTIPLIER EFFECT

Local redistribution through food banks and community organizations generates an
estimated economic multiplier of 1.3x, meaning each dollar of product value generates
approximately 1.30 dollars of community economic activity through reduced household
food expenditure, improved nutrition outcomes, and secondary local spending.

3. REQUIRED ACTIONS

Action 1: Maintain an updated geographic overlay of USDA Food Access Research Atlas
data within the routing decision engine. Refresh the overlay dataset at minimum annually
or whenever the USDA publishes revised tract classifications.

Action 2: Calculate and report the aggregate community resilience impact score for each
quarterly reporting period. The report must include total volume redistributed to
low-access tracts, estimated meals provided, and the weighted community resilience
subscore averaged across all routing decisions. Submit to the cooperative governance
board and make available to member stakeholders within 30 days of quarter close.
