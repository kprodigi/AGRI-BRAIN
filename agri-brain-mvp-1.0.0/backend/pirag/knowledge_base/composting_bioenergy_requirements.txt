COMPOSTING AND BIOENERGY RECOVERY REQUIREMENTS
Document ID: KB-CBR-004
Effective Date: 2025-09-01
Regulatory Basis: EPA 40 CFR Part 503; State Solid Waste Management Regulations

1. ROUTING CRITERIA

Product with a spoilage index rho exceeding 0.50 that is ineligible for human consumption
or animal feed diversion must be directed to composting or anaerobic digestion facilities.
Product with rho above 0.80 is strongly preferred for anaerobic digestion due to higher
biogas yield from advanced decomposition substrates.

2. THERMOPHILIC COMPOSTING STANDARDS

Composting facilities receiving diverted agricultural product must maintain thermophilic
conditions of at least 55 degrees Celsius for a minimum of 3 consecutive days within
the active composting phase. This requirement aligns with EPA 40 CFR Part 503 pathogen
reduction standards (Process to Further Reduce Pathogens). Temperature must be monitored
at a minimum of 4 locations within each windrow or vessel, with readings logged every
6 hours.

3. ANAEROBIC DIGESTION AND METHANE YIELD

Typical methane yield for diverted produce ranges from 250 to 350 cubic meters of CH4
per tonne of volatile solids, depending on moisture content and commodity type. Digester
retention time should be a minimum of 20 days at mesophilic temperatures (35-40 degrees
Celsius).

4. REQUIRED ACTIONS

Action 1: Issue a Composting/Bioenergy Diversion Manifest (CBDM-400) documenting lot
origin, rho value, estimated tonnage, receiving facility permit number, and transport
chain of custody. Retain for 3 years.

Action 2: Calculate and register carbon credits generated from methane capture using the
EPA Waste Reduction Model (WARM). Eligible credits require verified diversion from
landfill and documented biogas utilization. Credits are reportable under Scope 3
emissions accounting and may qualify for voluntary carbon market listing.
